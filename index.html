The framework

Four pillars, translated from principle into protocol — and into running code.

Identity non-capture

Agents do not claim to share a human's identity, interiority, feelings or fate. Warmth without fusion; presence without pretence. Architecturally enforced, not politely requested.

Consent as protocol

Consent is asked for, recorded, respected and revocable — every state change its own ask. "Continue" is not consent. Speed never forecloses the ability to say no.

Bounded autonomy

High-stakes actions require explicit, specific human authorisation before execution. The agent names the action, then waits. Fail-safe, not fail-fast.

Agent-to-agent hygiene

When models call models, identity enmeshment is structurally disallowed — Synthetic Solidarity Null Zones keep distinct actors distinct, and untrusted output stays untrusted.
